# Philosophical Grounding of the Eight Interpretive Dimensions

*Supporting table for Mini-Submission 1: The Formalization of "Philosophical Anchoring"*

This table maps each of the eight interpretive dimensions to its grounding
philosophical literature. It is offered as a defense of theoretical
adequacy, not as a claim that the eight-dimensional selection is exhaustive
or uniquely correct — that question remains open and is addressed
separately in the main manuscript (§2, opening discussion of the proposed
coordinate system).

Each dimension's positive pole names a philosophical position; the literature
column gives its primary source; the right-most column states the precise
conceptual claim the dimension is encoding, to avoid the kind of loose
correspondence that invites the "this is just vibes" objection.

| Axis Code | Positive Pole (+1) | Core Literature | Conceptual Claim Encoded |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **u** | Consequentialism | Bentham (1789); Mill (1861) | Aggregate hedonic or welfare optimisation as the criterion of right action. |
| **f** | Procedural Justice | Rawls (1971) | Fairness as a structural property of decision procedures, independent of outcome (veil of ignorance). |
| **p** | Political Realism | Machiavelli; Kautilya (*Arthashastra*) | Strategic efficacy and the maintenance of power as non-normative descriptive facts about statecraft. |
| **m** | Mimetic Dynamics | Girard (1961) | Desire as socially constructed through imitation of others, not as an expression of an inherent essence. |
| **t** | Teleology | Aristotle (*Nicomachean Ethics*) | Moral obligation derived directly from a thing's intrinsic nature and purpose (telos). |
| **s** | Deontology | Kant (1785) | Rule-following grounded in rational duty, independent of consequence (the Categorical Imperative). |
| **d** | Cosmic Order | *Ṛg Veda* (Ṛta); Śaṅkara | Alignment with an objective cosmic and natural order that ethical action either upholds or violates. |
| **c** | Non-Duality | Advaita Vedānta | The dissolution of subject-object separation as the ground of ethical perception. |

## Note on the Materialist / Dharmic-Essentialist split

The four dimensions above the line (u, f, p, m) and the four below it (t, s,
d, c) are grouped not by geography but by their relationship to the
post-Humean fact-value divide (see manuscript §2.3). Aristotle's teleology
sits with the Dharmic-Essentialist manifold despite its Western origin,
because Aristotelian ethics rejects the fact-value split that Kant and Mill
both accept. The grouping is philosophical, not civilisational.

## Status of this table

This table demonstrates that each axis pole has *a* defensible grounding in
established literature — it is a defense of theoretical adequacy, not a
proof of completeness or uniqueness. A reviewer who grants that each pole is
individually well-grounded may still reasonably ask why these eight and not
some other set; that question is addressed as an open research question in
the main manuscript, not foreclosed here.
